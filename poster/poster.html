The Problem

A professor walks up to a whiteboard. They write, explain, erase, correct, and move on. By the end of the lecture the board looks nothing like it did at the start. A photo of the final board captures the conclusion, missing everything that happened in between. We wanted to capture the full story.

✕ Students spend all their time copying and miss the actual explanation.
✕ When the professor erases to make room, that content is gone forever.
✕ There's no way to replay how an idea developed, only its final form survives.

How It Works

A camera feeds a 10-stage real-time pipeline. Models run in isolated subprocesses: the main loop never blocks on inference.

Video Feed
Queue(maxsize=1): stale frames dropped. Always the freshest.

Board Segmentation

Segments whiteboard region. Async subprocess · ~5 s cadence.

▼
Perspective Correction
Homography → canonical 1920×1080. All downstream geometry locked to this space.
▼
Surface Reconstruction
Distance-weighted EMA. Pixels under body frozen at last known value.
▼
Text Line Detection
PP-OCRv5 server det
Detects every text line on clean composite. Async subprocess.
▼
Block Grouping
Clusters lines into blocks. Pluggable strategy: Union-Find · HDBSCAN · AABB-Tree.
▼
Entity Registry
Tracks blocks across frames. IoU + centroid matching. EMA bbox. Stable 10 s → dispatch OCR.
▼
OCR Transcription
PaddleOCR-VL-1.5
VLM reads each stable entity crop. Async subprocess.
▼
Ledger Synthesis
Atomic overwrite of live.md + lecture_history.

System in Action

Output Files

live.md
Always up to date. Reflects exactly what is visible on the board right now.
lecture_history.md
Chronological record of the full lecture. Every block in appearance order, with revisions.

Every Block Has a Lifecycle

Once detected, a block lives through four states. Identity is spatial: a correction in-place is a new version of the same entity; a rewrite at a new location is a new entity entirely.

STABILIZING
new block, or drift reset
INFERRING
crop sent to VLM
ACTIVE
text written to ledger
ERASED
archived with timestamp · pruned after 3 s
Any movement, including edits to already-transcribed blocks, resets the stability clock.
